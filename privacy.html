Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2025

The short version

KnotQ is a fully local application. Your data stays on your device. We do not collect, transmit, or store any personal information, usage data, or analytics.

Data storage

All schemes, folders, tasks, calendar entries, and settings are stored locally on your computer in KnotQ's application data directory. No data is sent to any server. There is no account system and no cloud sync at this time.

Google Calendar integration

KnotQ offers an optional integration with Google Calendar. This integration is read-only: KnotQ can display events from your Google Calendar alongside your schemes, but it does not modify, create, or delete any events in your Google account.

When you connect a Google Calendar:

  • KnotQ uses Google's OAuth 2.0 flow to obtain a read-only access token.
  • Calendar event data is fetched directly from Google's API and stored locally on your device.
  • No calendar data is transmitted to KnotQ's servers or any third party.
  • You can disconnect your Google Calendar at any time from the Settings page, which revokes the stored token.

No analytics or tracking

KnotQ does not include any analytics, telemetry, crash reporting, or tracking of any kind. We do not use cookies, fingerprinting, or any other identification mechanism.

App store distribution

KnotQ is currently available as a direct download from GitHub. We plan to distribute KnotQ through the macOS App Store and Microsoft Store in the future. When distributed through these stores, the respective store's standard policies apply to the download and update process, but the app itself will continue to store all data locally.

Third-party services

Beyond the optional Google Calendar integration described above, KnotQ does not communicate with any third-party services.

Changes to this policy

If we make changes to this privacy policy, we will update this page and note the date of the last revision above.

Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy, please open an issue on the KnotQ GitHub repository.
